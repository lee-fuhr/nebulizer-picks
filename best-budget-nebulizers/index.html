If you or a family member relies on a nebulizer for asthma, COPD, or other respiratory conditions, finding an affordable yet effective unit is essential. A budget-friendly model doesn’t have to mean cutting corners on delivery, many compressors in this price range still provide consistent aerosol therapy, but features like noise level, portability, and cup design vary widely. To choose wisely, focus on the type of compressor (piston versus diaphragm), the size and weight if you plan to move it around, and how easy the medication cup is to clean. Also consider whether the kit includes extra accessories such as a mouthpiece or pediatric mask.
